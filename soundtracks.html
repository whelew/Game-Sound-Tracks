The Most Iconic SoundTracks

Undertale - Megalovania

Based in the key of D minor Megalovania begins with a highly infectious motif. It utilises the same melody pattern over a descending bass note, starting on D. It then descends down to C, B and then Bb. The track uses a large amount of synth to reflect the retro aesthetic that Undertale brings as a game. The track also features an electric guitar, using a live instrument really helps bring the synthetic instruments alive.

Hades - Good Riddance

Good Riddance stands apart from the rest of the Hades soundtrack. It is a hauntingly beautiful ballad, and captures the essence of the underworld. It makes use of beautiful chord movements, starting with a minor vi (cm) chord and landing on a major V (Bb). Followed by a minor ii (fm) to a Major I (Eb). Then finishing on a Major V to IV (Bb to Ab). Having such close chord movements allows for beautiful voice leading through the melody.

Rip and Tear - Doom

The Doom sountrack is by far one of the heaviest soundtracks that has ever been made for a game. The composer Mick Gordan uses an eight string electric guitar and makes great use of the musical style Djent. Focused around heavy riffs on the low F# and B strings using interesting rhythmic patterns. Rip and Tear captures what Doom is all about, it is brutal, heavy and perfectly immerses the player into the game.

Gwyn, Lord of Cinder - Dark Souls

This is one of my favourite pieces, based around two simple motifs, A and B. The first motif melody plays a simple 5, 4, 1 followed by a variation of a 5, 4, b7 before stepping back down to the starting note on the 5. The motif has a lot of purposeful space to reflect the character Gwyn. If you are not familiar with the story of Gwyn, he is soldier left fighting for his beliefs, and instead of an epic boss fight track, we are given this somber piece of music, making the player almost pity this character.

Minecraft

Minecraft has a very minimalistic and ambient soundtrack, the music is the perfect compliment to the game. Brian Eno a composer when talking about enviromental music says it needs to be Ignorable as it is Interesting. The Minecraft soundtrack does exactly that, it is simple, with slow chord movements and very simple melodies.
